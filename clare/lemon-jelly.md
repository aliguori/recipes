---
title: Lemon Jelly
servings: 4–5 Half-Pints
prep: 45 Min
cook: 20 Min
---

## Ingredients

### Clarified Juice
- 8 cups freshly squeezed lemon juice, strained
- agar-agar powder (see step 1)

### Jelly
- 4 cups clarified lemon juice
- ¾ cup sugar
- 4 tsp Pomona's pectin powder
- 4 tsp Pomona's calcium water

## Directions

1. **Clarify:** Weigh the lemon juice. Measure water equal to ¼ of that weight, and agar equal to 0.2% of the juice weight.
2. Whisk the agar into the water, boil while stirring, and simmer a couple minutes to hydrate.
3. While briskly whisking the boiling agar, slowly add the lemon juice in a thin stream.
4. Set the mixture over an ice bath to gel, then gently whisk it into agar curds.
5. Pour into a cheesecloth-lined strainer; lift and gently squeeze to drain the clear juice, stirring the curds to release more. Re-strain if needed.
6. **Make the jelly:** Combine 4 cups clarified juice with the calcium water in a pot.
7. Mix the sugar and pectin powder thoroughly in a separate bowl.
8. Boil the juice, add the pectin-sugar, and stir vigorously 1–2 minutes to dissolve. Return to a boil and remove from heat.
9. Ladle into hot jars, leaving ¼″ headspace. Process in a boiling water bath 10 minutes.
