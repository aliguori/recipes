---
title: Decoux Family Gumbo
servings: 1 Large Pot
prep: Roux 2–3 Hrs
total: ~6 Hrs
---

## Ingredients

### Roux
- 6 heaping spoonfuls flour
- ~1 cup oil (or skimmed gumbo grease)
- water

### Gumbo
- ~2 cups cooked roux (4 cups for two chickens)
- 3 banana peppers, seeds in
- 1½ onions
- 3 heaping tbsp dried parsley flakes
- ~1 cup green onion tops
- 1½ tbsp salt
- cayenne, to taste
- smoked beef or pork sausage
- 1 large chicken, cut up, plus extra drumsticks
- water

## Directions

1. **Make the roux:** Scoop 6 heaping spoonfuls of flour into a heavy skillet and add ~1 cup oil to form a thick paste. Cook and stir on low until a rich chocolate color, 45 minutes to 1½ hours. If it turns black or smells burnt, discard and start over.
2. Remove any oil on top, add a little water, and simmer uncovered 1½ to 2 hours. This makes enough for several gumbos; store with a little oil on top in the fridge, or freeze indefinitely.
3. **Make the gumbo:** Put an inch of water in a large deep pot and add ~2 cups cooked roux. Cook and stir, adding more roux as needed, until a rich brown color — it tends to stick to the bottom.
4. Puree the banana peppers and onions with a little water; add to the gumbo along with the parsley, green onion tops, salt, and cayenne. Cook about 20 minutes.
5. Add the sausage, cut-up chicken, and extra drumsticks. Add more water, partially cover, and cook on low until the chicken is done, skimming as it cooks. (Turkey necks also make a good gumbo.)
